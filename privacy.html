3. Purpose and Legal Basis for Processing

  • Contract performance (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)): providing platform access, processing tasks, handling payments and internal coin transactions.
  • Legitimate interests (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)): platform security, fraud prevention, anti-money-laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) checks, analytics for service improvement.
  • Legal obligations (GDPR Art. 6(1)(c)): retention of financial records as required under EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive 2015/849 (AMLD5), applicable tax legislation, and national financial-reporting requirements.
  • Consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)): marketing communications (only where explicit opt-in consent has been obtained; you may withdraw consent at any time pursuant to GDPR Art. 7(3)).

4. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described herein and in compliance with applicable retention periods:

  • Account data: retained for the duration of the account relationship plus 5 years (per AMLD5, Art. 40).
  • Financial/transaction records: retained for a minimum of 5 years following the transaction, or longer as required by national law.
  • Server logs: retained for up to 90 days.
  • Marketing consent records: retained for 3 years from last interaction or until consent withdrawal.

5. Data Security Measures

In accordance with GDPR Article 32, we implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including but not limited to:

  • Passwords stored exclusively as bcrypt hashes (never in plaintext).
  • Session data protected by HttpOnly and Secure cookie flags.
  • All data transmitted over HTTPS/TLS 1.2+ encrypted connections.
  • CSRF protection on all state-changing API endpoints.
  • Rate limiting to mitigate brute-force and abuse attacks.
  • Principle of least privilege applied to database access and API permissions.

6. Third-Party Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. Data may be shared with:

  • Infrastructure providers (hosting, databases) — strictly to the extent necessary for service operation, bound by Data Processing Agreements per GDPR Art. 28.
  • Payment processors — for the purpose of processing financial transactions.
  • Law enforcement / government authorities — only when legally compelled by valid court order, subpoena, or applicable law (e.g., GDPR Art. 6(1)(c); CCPA § 1798.145(a)).

We do not transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA) unless adequate safeguards are in place in accordance with GDPR Chapter V (Arts. 44–49), such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted by the European Commission.

7. Cryptocurrency and Blockchain Data

Blockchain transactions are public and irreversible by nature. We store transaction hashes and wallet addresses for verification and support purposes. You are solely responsible for the confidentiality and security of your private keys. We are not liable for any loss resulting from compromised private keys, incorrect addresses, or irreversible blockchain transactions.

Applicable law: EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) — Regulation (EU) 2023/1114; Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations 15–16 (Travel Rule).

Lolanceizi is not intended for persons under the age of 18 (or the minimum legal age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If we discover that data has been collected from a minor, we will take immediate steps to delete it in accordance with GDPR Art. 8 and the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), 15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506.

Where data is transferred outside the EEA, we rely on: (a) European Commission adequacy decisions (GDPR Art. 45); (b) Standard Contractual Clauses (GDPR Art. 46(2)(c)); or (c) binding corporate rules (GDPR Art. 47). For transfers to the United States, we comply with the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework where applicable.

12. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours (GDPR Art. 33) and affected individuals without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms (GDPR Art. 34).
